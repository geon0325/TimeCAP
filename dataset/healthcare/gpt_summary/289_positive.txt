Over the past 20 weeks, there has been variability in the number of respiratory specimens testing positive for influenza, with an overall average positivity rate of 6.26%. During this period, the number of specimens tested reached its peak in week 20 with 41,117 tests, while the lowest testing volume was in week 10 at 22,366. The positivity ratio for Influenza A and B fluctuated modestly, with Influenza A peaking at 0.08% in week 5 and Influenza B reaching its highest at 0.09% in week 6. The combined positivity ratio for both influenza types peaked at 0.15% in weeks 5 and 6, indicating a slight surge in influenza cases during this period. Considering the observed trends, healthcare providers should remain vigilant for potential increases in influenza activity and continue to promote vaccinations, especially during periods where an upward trend in positivity rates is detected.
