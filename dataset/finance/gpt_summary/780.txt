Over the last 20 market days, the S&P 500 exhibited a downward trend, closing lower by 3.8% from its starting point, suggesting a bearish market sentiment. The VIX, or Fear Index, has trended upwards, reflecting increased market volatility and uncertainty among investors, ending 37.8% higher. In international markets, the Nikkei 225 also declined by 5.3%, while the FTSE 100 experienced a significant drop of 7%, indicating widespread cautiousness in equity markets.

Gold futures rose by 8.8%, potentially signaling a flight to safety as investors sought more stable assets amid heightened volatility. Contrasting the trend in equities and indicating supply concerns or increased demand, Crude Oil futures surged by 25.2%, which could contribute to inflationary pressures. The exchange rates show a slight strengthening of the USD against the EUR and JPY but remained relatively stable against the CNY, suggesting that currency markets may be less volatile compared to equities and commodities.
