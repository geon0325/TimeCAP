Over the last 20 market days, the S&P 500 experienced a significant decline, dropping from 3352.09 to 2746.56, indicating increased market uncertainty and a potential bearish outlook. The VIX, or Volatility Index, more than tripled from 15.04 to 54.46, reflecting this heightened uncertainty and investor fear. Crude Oil Futures plummeted from 49.57 to 31.13, likely due to concerns over global demand and a potential price war. Exchange rates showed a marked depreciation of the US dollar against the Euro and the Japanese Yen, moving from 1.09 to 1.14 for EUR/USD and from 109.64 to 103.94 for USD/JPY, indicating a shift in currency strength dynamics.
