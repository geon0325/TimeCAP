Over the past 20 weeks, the mortality from Influenza or Pneumonia as a percentage of total deaths has generally decreased, starting at 9.28% and ending at 5.75%, which is below the 20-week average of 7.84%. The total number of deaths has also shown a declining trend from 68,969 to 56,592. Deaths from influenza have seen a significant decline from 887 to 33, while pneumonia-related deaths have decreased from 5,516 to 3,220 over the same period. The most substantial decrease in the mortality ratio of Influenza or Pneumonia occurred between the first and last week, with a drop of 3.53 percentage points, indicating a potential improvement in the management or prevalence of these conditions. However, the persistent presence of these diseases and their contribution to mortality underscores the need for ongoing surveillance and healthcare resource allocation to manage and prevent these respiratory illnesses.
