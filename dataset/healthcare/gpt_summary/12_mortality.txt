Over the last 20 weeks, there has been a noticeable downward trend in the ratio of mortality from Influenza or Pneumonia compared to the total number of deaths, starting at 7.58% and ending at 5.43%, which is below the average ratio of 7.84%. This decrease suggests an improvement in the management or severity of these respiratory diseases. The total number of deaths fluctuated, with a slight decrease overall from 55,703 to 50,237. There was a significant drop in the number of deaths from influenza, from 333 to 6, indicating a potential reduction in influenza transmission or effectiveness of prevention measures. Concurrently, deaths from pneumonia also decreased from 3,887 to 2,723, reinforcing the trend of a decline in mortality from these conditions, which could alleviate some of the burden on healthcare resources dedicated to these illnesses.
